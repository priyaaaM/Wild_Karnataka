Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Chikmagalur District, Karnataka

Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary, located in the picturesque Chikmagalur district of Karnataka, is a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. Situated in the Western Ghats, this sanctuary spans over 490 square kilometers and is known for its rich biodiversity, including tigers, leopards, elephants, and a variety of birds. The sanctuary is also home to several species of flora and fauna, making it a perfect retreat for anyone looking to experience the natural beauty of the region.... The sanctuary derives its name from the Bhadra River, which flows through the region and adds to its scenic beauty. Visitors can enjoy a thrilling wildlife safari, trekking, and birdwatching in this lush green forest, which is also a part of the Western Ghats UNESCO World Heritage site. Bhadra is one of the best places in Karnataka for spotting tigers, and it is also home to a large population of wild elephants. The sanctuary offers a pristine environment with dense forests, valleys, and hills that are rich in biodiversity. If you're lucky, you might spot a majestic tiger, elephants grazing near the river, or a variety of exotic birds in their natural habitat.

These are just a few highlights about Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary, located in the Chikmagalur district of Karnataka.
