Nagarahole National Park

Located in Kodagu and Mysuru, Karnataka

Nagarahole National Park, also known as Rajiv Gandhi National Park, is one of the most famous wildlife sanctuaries located in the southern part of Karnataka. Nestled between the Kodagu and Mysuru districts, the park covers an area of about 643 square kilometers and is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including tigers, elephants, leopards, and sloth bears. The park's dense forests, winding rivers, and diverse wildlife make it a favorite destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.... The park is named after the Nagarahole River, which flows through it, and is part of the larger Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. Nagarahole is known for its well-preserved forest cover and offers visitors the opportunity to experience a thrilling safari through its forests. Apart from the Big Five, the park also hosts numerous species of birds, reptiles, and aquatic life, making it a biodiversity hotspot. If you're lucky, you might spot a majestic tiger or a herd of wild elephants while on safari. Nagarahole is also a haven for birdwatchers, with over 270 species of birds recorded here.

These are just a few highlights about Nagarahole National Park, located in Kodagu and Mysuru, Karnataka.
