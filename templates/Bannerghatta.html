Bannerghatta Forest

Located near Bangalore, Karnataka

Bannerghatta Forest is a renowned wildlife sanctuary located just 22 kilometers south of Bangalore, Karnataka. The sanctuary is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, offering an ideal retreat for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. Established in 1971, the forest spans an area of 104.27 square kilometers and is known for its rich biodiversity, including tigers, elephants, leopards, and several species of birds and reptiles.... The forest is part of the larger Bannerghatta National Park, which includes a zoo, a butterfly park, and a safari park, making it one of the most visited eco-tourism destinations in Karnataka. Visitors can enjoy a thrilling safari experience, spotting various animals in their natural habitat. The park also has a rescue center for rescued wild animals. Whether you're interested in birdwatching, wildlife photography, or simply enjoying the serenity of the forest, Bannerghatta has something for everyone. The area is also home to a number of temples and scenic viewpoints, adding to its charm as a cultural and natural destination.

These are just a few highlights about Bannerghatta Forest, located near Bangalore, Karnataka.
